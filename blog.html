Understanding Pipe Relining

Pipe relining, often referred to as 'Cured-In-Place Pipelining (CIPP)', is a trenchless rehabilitation method used to repair existing pipelines. This technique involves inserting a 'liner' – typically made of a resin-saturated felt tube – into the damaged pipe, effectively creating a new pipe within the old one. This method is not only efficient but also minimises the need for excavation, preserving your landscape and property structure.

Why Choose Pipe Relining Solutions?

  1. Minimal Disruption

    2. Unlike traditional methods that require extensive digging and excavation, pipe relining solutions offer a no-dig alternative. This means less noise, less mess, and less inconvenience for property owners and residents.

  2. Cost-Effective

    3. With pipe relining services, the overall cost is often lower than traditional pipe replacement. The process is faster, requires fewer workers, and cuts the landscape restoration costs.

  3. Longevity and Durability

    4. The materials used in pipe relining are resistant to corrosion, tree-root intrusions, and have a lifespan that rivals, if not exceeds, traditional piping materials.

FAQs

What is pipe relining and how does it work?
Pipe relining is a no-dig method to repair damaged pipes by inserting a resin-saturated liner, creating a new, durable pipe within the old one.
How long does pipe relining last?
Pipe relining is highly durable, typically lasting for 50 years or more, depending on the materials used and the installation quality.
Is pipe relining more cost-effective than traditional pipe replacement?
Yes, pipe relining is generally more cost-effective due to minimal excavation, reduced labour, and lower restoration costs.
